AVAIL Zone Selection Tool

The AVAIL Zone Selection tool is designed to assist with the selection and editing of the geographies to be used when modeling each of the 5 zones for the Integration of Bus Stop Count Data with Census data for Improving Bus Service and Efficiency RFP 2012-13 project. NJ Transit, in conjunction with AVAIL, will select the census geographies for each zone based on experience and available data.

1.      Web Location

To access the Zone Selector tool, navigate your browser to http://njtransit.AVAILabs.org/njtransit . We recommend using Google Chrome or Mozilla Firefox.

 

Figure 1 - Zone Selector Tool Web Location

1.1.   Login Information.

When attempting to access the above website, the user will be prompted to enter login credentials. This is implemented to restrict access, and can be modified to meet your requirements.

2.      Map Navigation

Once logged in to the Zone Selector Tool, you will be presented with a map showing the Census Tracts of New Jersey.

2.1.   Pan

The movement controls on this map behave similarly to other web-based mapping websites. There is a four-way Pan control in the top left corner of the map pane. This can be used to move the map up, down, left and right.

Figure 3 - Pan Control

Panning can also be done by Dragging the map with your mouse cursor by clicking and holding, then sliding the map in the direction you wish. NOTE: Manual panning will not work once a zone layer is selected, and the Pan controls must be used.

2.2.   Zoom

Zooming can be performed several different ways. Immediately below the Pan controls is the Zoom control slider. Slide up to zoom in, and down to zoom out. Alternately, you can use your mouse wheel to zoom. Wheel forward to zoom in and wheel backwards to zoom out.

 

Figure 4 - Zoom Control Slider

2.3.   Drag-Zoom

Another means of zooming is with the Drag-Zoom feature. This allows the user to select a specific area, and zoom in to that location. This is done simply by holding down the SHIFT key while holding down the left mouse button and dragging the cursor over the area you wish to inspect. This will create a box covering the area. Release the mouse button to zoom once you have covered the desired area.

Figure 5 - The Drag-Zoom Feature

 

2.4.   Zoom Reset

If desired, at any point the Zoom level can be reset to its initial settings. To accomplish this, press the Zoom To Full State button at the lop left corner of the page.

 

Figure 6 - Zoom Reset

3.      Base layer Options

A base layer is the map that shows up behind the zones. We have included a number of different options to allow for some comparison. In most instances, Open Street Maps should be sufficient, but we have included Google base layers as well. To choose between different base layers, you will need to access the layers panel by clicking the small directional arrow on the right side of the screen, in the middle.


Figure 7 - Hide/Show Layers Button - Base Layer Selection

 

The currently available base layers are as follows:

         Open Street Maps (Default)

         Google Views

o   Satellite

o   Hybrid

o   Streets

4.      Zone Selection

The selection of the geographic areas to be included within each zone is meant to provide a baseline area for modeling purposes. On the map, each zone is given a unique color, and its census geographies can be selected and deselected for modeling purposes.

 

NOTE: The zone selections are automatically saved and synchronized between sessions. We recommend that only one person modify zones at any given time.

4.1.    5 Zones

         Newark Large Urban Area - Red

         Paterson Small Urban Area - Blue

         Atlantic City South Jersey Urban Center - Green

         Philadelphia Intercity NJ Market - Purple

         Princeton West Windsor Plainsboro Suburban Center - Orange

 

NOTE: Census Geographies can be included in multiple Zones. If this happens, the colors will blend and the particular geographies will be included in the models for multiple zones.

 

Figure 8 A possible Philadelphia - Intercity NJ Market Zone

4.1.1.      Selecting/Deselecting Geographies

When you first navigate to the Zone Selector Tool, the screen you are presented with is split with a left panel and a right panel. The right panel contains the map and map controls, while the left panel contains the 5 Zones, and information specific to each one. It will look similar to the screen capture below;

 

Figure 9 - Initial Zone Selector View

Each area listed in the left panel corresponds to its colored zone on the map in the right panel. Left-Clicking on an area will automatically zoom the map to its zone. Doing this also brings up more information about the area, and access to the GTFS information. Right now we will ignore the GTFS options.

 

 

Figure 10 - With an Area selected, the map is automatically zoomed.

 

Once zoomed in to the selected area, Census Geographies can be selected and deselected to add them to the zone being used for modeling.

4.1.2.      Adding a Zone


Adding a zone to an area can be accomplished by left-clicking on the census geography that you wish you add.

 

Figure 11 - Left-Clicking on the Census Tract Adds it to the Chosen Area.

4.1.3.      Removing a Zone


Removing a zone is very similar to adding a zone. To remove a Census Geography depress the CTRL key on your keyboard while left clicking on the map.

 

 

4.2.    GTFS Routes

When the GTFS Button is selected for a specific area, the application loads a subset of the full state GTFS provided by NJ Transit Developer Resources (http://njtransit.com/developer) specific to the area that you have selected. This subset is displayed on top of the selected Census Geographies on the right-side map panel. The thickness of each line is related to the number of trips associated with each route.

 

Figure 12 - AVAIL Zone Selector Tool With GTFS Selected

 

To help correlate the tabular GTFS Route List with the map display, hovering your mouse cursor over an entry on the table will highlight that route in neon green on the map.

 

Figure 13 - GTFS Display with Mouseover Highlight

 

The left panel displays information about each route. The number of trips associated with each route as well as the number of different shapes each route can take. The shapes correlate to the different route of travel that each bus route takes. This is based off of the GTFS information referenced prior

4.2.1.      Selecting/Deselecting GTFS Routes

To select a GTFS route for inclusion in the zone for each area, check the box at the left of the desired route number.

 

Figure 14 - Display with GTFS Routes Selected.

 

Once a route is selected in the left panel, it will turn red in the map panel to indicate that it is selected.

 

To deselect a route, uncheck the box at the left of the desired route number.
